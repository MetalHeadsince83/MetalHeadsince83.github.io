Death Magnetic

2008, the year Metallica released "Death Magnetic" The album featured more complex song structure than St. Anger, including guitar solos and a return to the thrash metal stylings of their first 4 albums. With Death Magnetic, Metallica recalled a classic era without sounding like a parody of themselves. The album cover of Metallica's "Death Magnetic" is a subtle design with shards of metal arranged in a curious pattern. The album was released in 2008 and was produced by Rick Rubin, marking the band's first album since "Metallica" (1991) not to be produced by longtime collaborator Bob Rock. The cover art has been described as a tribute to people who have fallen in the music business, with some being drawn towards death and others being afraid of it. The album brought more songs like "The Day That Never Comes" and "Unforgiven III" and "Cyanide"
