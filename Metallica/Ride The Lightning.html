Ride The Lightning

Oh... Good 'ol 1984, the year that Metallica's "Ride The Lightning" released. Ride the Lightning is a Metallica album whose title was taken from a passage in Stephen King's novel The Stand, in which a character uses the phrase to refer to execution by electric chair. The artwork, based on a concept by the band, depicts an electric chair being struck by lightning flowing from the band logo. The phrase "Ride the Lightning" also seemed to describe Metallica's musical approach, and the group soon adopted it as a suitable title for its new album This brought more to the table of iconic songs like "Ride The Lightning" or "Fade To Black" or "Creeping Death" This album had two more songs on it: "Blitzkrieg" and "Am I Evil?" but they were taken out and put on their album "Garage, Inc." in 1998.
