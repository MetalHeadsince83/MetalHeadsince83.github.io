Kill 'Em All

1983, the year that started it all. Metallica's first album "Kill 'Em All" was released. The album cover for Metallica's "Kill 'Em All" was designed by the band themselves. The title was a response to distributors who were trying to censor them. The artwork, done by Gary Heard, depicted blood stains, a sledgehammer, and the shadow of a hand, introducing an element of gore and violence. The album had began Metallica's journey with many iconic songs like "Seek And Destroy" or "Motorbreath" or "The Four Horseman" or "Hit The Lights"
