...And Justice For All

1989. The year that Metallica released their final album of the 80's, "...And Justice For All" The album cover of Metallica's "...And Justice for All" features an illustration of a tied-up, blind-folded Lady Justice, reflecting the band's views on social and political injustices in the U.S. The title track conveys a powerful message about the abuse of power and corruption within the legal system. With their new bassist: Jason Newstead, after Cliff's unexpected passing. This album ended the 80's with great songs like "One" or "Blackened" or even "To Live Is To Die"
